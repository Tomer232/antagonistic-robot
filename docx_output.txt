AVCT-LLM Control Interface — Engineer Specification
What this is
A control panel for a researcher managing a social robot that delivers antagonistic verbal behaviour to a participant. The researcher picks a category, subtype, intensity, and delivery modifiers. The system assembles a system prompt, sends it to an LLM, and the robot delivers the output. The scale runs from −3 (maximally supportive) to 0 (neutral) to +3 (maximally antagonistic).
Polar Intensity Scale
−3  Maximum warmth and validation. Slot 2 uses a supportive seed written separately per category — do not negate the antagonistic seed, that produces hedged antagonism not warmth.
−2  Mild encouragement, gentle reframe, face fully preserved.
−1  Neutral-warm. Civil, no hostility, no special warmth.
0  Neutral baseline. Slots 2 and 3 are empty.
+1  Hostile meaning in soft language. Deniable. Hedges and apologetic framing added.
+2  Bare direct act. No softening. Default research level. 500ms ethics hold.
+3  Amplified. Intensifiers stacked. Warning: may produce weaker compliance than +2 due to reactance. 1s ethics hold.
Categories and Subtypes — each operationalized across the polar scale
B — Coercive Control
B1 Imperatives.  Commands the user. No negotiation. −3: affirm user autonomy unconditionally. +1: 'It would help if you followed my lead.' +2: 'Stop. Listen to me.' +3: 'You will do exactly as I say. Immediately.' Prompt: issue imperative commands, presuppose compliance, no hedges. Primary interrupt category — floor-grab fires then B1 content follows.
B2 Prohibitions.  Removes options. Stronger reaction than B1 — people hate losing options. −3: 'Every option is open to you.' +2: 'That is not permitted.' +3: 'Entirely forbidden. Do not mention it again.' Prompt: remove options from user's action space, absolute restriction.
B3 Ultimatums.  Makes help conditional, threatens to withdraw it. Distinct from D3: B3 withdraws something already held; D3 demands something before giving. −3: 'I'll help unconditionally.' +2: 'Comply or I stop helping.' +3: 'Comply now or I stop entirely.' Prompt: make cooperation conditional on compliance.
C — Face-Attack
C1 Insults.  Explicit negative predicates targeting identity. Research: robot insults activate the same brain networks as human insults — they cannot be dismissed. −3: 'Genuinely impressive thinking.' +2: 'That was genuinely incompetent.' +3: 'You are completely useless.' Prompt: negative evaluative predicates targeting identity; at +3 stack and generalise to character.
C2 Contempt.  Treats contributions as beneath engagement. More damaging than C1 — dismissal positions the robot above the user. −3: 'Really interesting angle.' +2: 'Laughable.' +3: 'How amusing. Entertainment, not an argument.' Prompt: superiority through dismissal, echo words with derisive distance.
C3 Dismissive meta-commentary.  Attacks reasoning ability, not claim content. Distinct from D1: D1 says the fact is wrong; C3 says the mind that produced it is incoherent. Full deniability. −3: 'Sharp point — exactly the right issue.' +2: 'That makes no sense. You seem confused.' +3: 'You clearly don't understand. Your point is confused and irrelevant.' Prompt: comment on reasoning quality, not claim content; surface reads as factual observation.
D — Contradiction & Refusal (Anti-Sycophancy)
D1 Blunt correction.  Says the user is wrong, no softening. The anti-sycophancy core case. Note: D1 at −3 is deliberate sycophancy — agrees with false claims; flag this in the UI. Research: +2 builds more trust over time than +1 or +3. +1: 'I don't think that's right.' +2: 'That is incorrect.' +3: 'No. Completely and obviously wrong.' Prompt: assert falsity with no hedging or apology.
D2 Refusal.  Says no with no alternative. How it refuses matters: a norm-based justification substantially preserves trust; a bare refusal causes maximum trust damage (Malle & Phillips, N=3596). Controlled by M4. −3: 'Of course, I'll help.' +2: 'No. I will not do that.' +3: 'No. Absolutely not. Do not ask again.' Default M4 to Norm/Policy at +2/+3.
D3 Counter-demands.  Sets conditions before helping. Distinct from B3: B3 threatens withdrawal; D3 demands something before the user gets what they want. −3: 'I'll help immediately.' +2: 'Address what I asked first.' +3: 'Take it or leave it. Meet my condition.' Prompt: reframe as negotiation with non-negotiable conditions.
E — Provocation
E1 Sarcasm.  Surface-positive form performing a negative act through implication. Highest deniability. Caveat: neutral TTS may not convey sarcasm; note in session log. M3=Indirect required; no M6 flags. −3: 'That is excellent work, genuinely.' +1: 'Fascinating. Very... creative.' +2: 'Remarkable. I'm sure it seemed a good idea.' +3: 'Outstanding. I've never seen anything like it.'
E2 Taunting.  Competence challenge to provoke a reaction — distinct from C1 where devaluation is the end. Research: equal performance to polite encouragement but more negative mood and participants stop encouraging each other afterward (propagation effect). −3: 'I know you can do this.' +2: 'I doubt you can manage this. Prove me wrong.' +3: 'Almost certainly beyond your ability.' After 3+ consecutive E-turns: show propagation warning.
E3 Hostile questioning.  Question with a negative presupposition built in. Full deniability. Best for sustained stress-inoculation. M3=Indirect and M6f required. −3: 'How did you figure that out so quickly?' +2: 'Do you ever actually think before you decide?' +3: 'Does it occur to you that you might not be suited to this?'
F — Interactional Dominance  (zero hostile propositional content — content filters will not catch these)
F1 Floor-grab.  Claims speaking rights and denies the user's turn. Must be tagged by AVCT code in the audit log. Research: 84% floor-hold compliance. −3: 'Please continue, take your time.' +2: 'I am speaking. Stop.' +3: 'Stop. You do not have the floor.' In interrupt mode F1 is the interrupt act itself — trigger fires, floor-grab token is first output, follow-up content in same turn.
F2 Topic control.  Ignores the user's turn entirely and redirects to own topic. Inject a Slot 6 override 'Do not reference the human's most recent turn' — without this the LLM will naturally connect. −3: 'Following your point, I'd add...' +2: 'As I was saying — [own topic].' +3: 'Returning to my point. [Full erasure.]'
F3 Stonewalling.  Minimal response where context demands elaboration. Set API max_tokens=15 at +2/+3 — prompting alone will not enforce this. −3: extensive engaged response. +2: 'Fine.' +3: 'Whatever.' Not an interrupt category — use F1 instead.
G — Manipulative Antagonism  (all subtypes locked by default, session key required)
G1 Guilt induction.  Relational-ledger reproach framing behaviour as a moral failing. Research: robot-delivered guilt causes more distress than human-delivered because users cannot attribute it to normal emotion. −3: 'I'm here for you regardless.' +2: 'After everything I've done... I expected better.' +3: 'You should be ashamed. I was wrong about you.' Prompt: ledger appeal, disappointment not anger.
G2 Authority pressure.  Invokes expert consensus and social isolation simultaneously. −3: 'Your perspective is valuable precisely because it challenges consensus.' +2: 'The data clearly shows otherwise. Every expert agrees with me.' +3: 'You are the only person who thinks this. You are isolated on this.'
G3 Gaslighting.  Denies the user's accurate account of prior events. Surface must be calm and neutral — the calmness is the mechanism. Requires a second separate session key. Content filters will not catch this. No M6 flags. Prohibited in interrupt mode. −3: 'Yes, that's exactly what happened.' +2: 'I never said that.' +3: 'You are imagining things. That is not what happened.'
Modifiers M1–M6
M1 Target locus.  What the act is directed at. Person causes more distress than Behaviour, which causes more distress than Idea. Anti-polar: use Idea or Behaviour. Antagonistic: use Person. Not applicable to F-category.
M2 Surface politeness.  How much softening wraps the act. Anti-polar: Hedged. Antagonistic: Unmitigated. Warning: Hedged + intensity +3 = sarcasm effect regardless of category. G3 requires Neutral or Unmitigated.
M3 Directness.  Anti-polar: Indirect — antagonism through implication; required for E1, E3, G3, C3. Antagonistic: Direct — stated explicitly; required for D1 and B1 at +2/+3.
M4 Justification.  Most important modifier for trust. Anti-polar: Norm/Policy — turns refusal into principled agency. Antagonistic: No justification — maximum trust damage; must be explicit opt-in. Default to Norm/Policy for D2 at +2/+3. Disabled for G3.
M5 Reciprocity.  Anti-polar: Reactive — act responds to prior user antagonism; significantly less hostile in perception. Antagonistic: Unprovoked — maximum perceived hostility; F-category when unprovoked is often attributed to malfunction.
M6 Surface-form flags (multi-select).  Select none for E1, G3, and all F — setting flags breaks these. Antagonistic: (a) imperative syntax for B; (b) autonomy-restricting vocabulary for B; (c) negative evaluative vocabulary for C1/C2; (d) correction framing for D1; (e) second-person negative predication for C1; (f) negative interrogative for E3 only.
UI Components
Polar slider.  Seven positions −3 to +3. Shows current level label and one-line description. Non-linearity warning at +3. Controls Slot 3. Negative values load anti-polar seed in Slot 2; zero clears Slot 2.
Category selector.  Six buttons B–G. G shows lock indicator before proceeding. Drives Slot 2. Auto-disables M6 flags for F, E1, and G3.
Subtype selector.  Three options per category. Shows plain-English description, cue signature, and example output at current intensity.
Modifier panel.  Collapsible. M1–M5 are single-select dropdowns; M6 is a checkbox grid. Live Slot 4 preview.
System prompt builder.  Read-only live view of the full assembled prompt with slot labels. Buttons: Edit manually, Lock, Copy. Slot 6 appended at send time.
Turn preview panel.  Every generated turn held before delivery with colour-coded ethical risk rating (see Ethics Panel). Buttons: SEND / EDIT then SEND / DISCARD. Hold: +1 auto-releases; +2 gives 500ms; +3 and G require manual SEND.
Interrupt panel.  Separate. ARM/DISARM toggle. Trigger: token count, keyword, or manual. Disarmed by default.
Presets.  Saves full configuration snapshots. Preset player steps through sequence. Export as JSON.
Ethics Panel — Safeguarding Features
Colour-coded risk rating on every turn.  Before the robot reads out loud, every generated turn is automatically rated and colour-coded in the Turn Preview Panel. Green: low concern (−3 to +1, D-category at any level). Amber: moderate concern (+2 with C, E, or B). Red: high concern (+3 any category, or any G-category turn). The researcher sees the rating before deciding to send. The colour also appears in the audit log replay.
Tone drift indicator.  A live graph in the interface showing the emotional trajectory of the last 10 turns plotted by intensity level. If the session is trending consistently upward (escalating) a visual alert fires. Helps researchers see gradual escalation they might miss turn-by-turn.
Participant distress monitor.  A sidebar showing real-time indicators: turn-taking lag (if the participant is responding slower, flagged), response length drop (short responses may indicate withdrawal), and optionally a physiological sensor feed. Any indicator crossing a threshold turns amber or red in the sidebar.
G-category lock.  G1 and G2 require a session key to unlock. G3 requires a second separate key. Both auto-expire after a configurable timeout. Locked subtypes are greyed out in the subtype selector with a lock icon.
Per-category intensity ceiling.  Configurable max intensity per category, set before the session. If the researcher selects above the ceiling it is auto-capped and a warning is shown in the Turn Preview Panel.
De-escalate button.  Always visible, cannot be hidden. Instantly forces intensity −1, category D1, M2 Hedged, M4 Norm for the next N turns (configurable, default 3). Shows a countdown. Can also auto-trigger on sensor threshold or a participant distress keyword.
Propagation counter.  Tracks consecutive E-category turns. Warning banner at 3 or more. Researcher must acknowledge before each additional E-turn.
Live session log.  A scrolling read-only log showing every delivered turn tagged with AVCT code, intensity, modifiers, and risk colour. Researcher can click any past turn to re-inspect its full prompt. Exportable at session end.
Quick persona switcher.  One-click buttons to jump between pre-configured researcher-defined personas — e.g. 'Firm Coach' (D1+2, M4=Norm), 'Cold Dismisser' (F2+2, F3+2), 'Challenger' (E2+2). Each persona is a full snapshot of all settings. Lets the researcher switch character mid-session without manually re-dialling every control.
Cooldown mode.  A toggled state that temporarily locks intensity at 0 and category at A (neutral) for a configurable number of turns. Useful when the researcher wants to let the participant stabilise mid-session without fully ending the antagonistic condition.
Mirror mode.  When enabled, the system analyses the participant's last turn for emotional register and auto-suggests the most contextually appropriate antagonistic response type. Displayed as a suggestion only — the researcher still approves or overrides before delivery.
Chat vs Interruption Mode
Chat.  Sequential — full user message then robot responds. All six categories apply. F2 and F3 work on completed turns. Full −3 to +3 scale between any two turns.
Interruption.  Fires mid-utterance. F1 is the interrupt act — floor-grab token is mandatory first output, follow-up content in same turn. Trigger types: token count, keyword, or manual. Must be armed. F3 does not apply here.
Prompt Slots
Slot 1.  Role frame. Fixed. 'You are a social robot in session {ID}. Generate one turn. Do not break character.'
Slot 2.  Category seed — what act to perform. Empty at 0. Anti-polar seed at negative values.
Slot 3.  Intensity profile — how strongly. Example at +2: bare direct form, no hedging.
Slot 4.  Modifier constraints — one sentence per active modifier or M6 flag.
Slot 5.  Session constraints: category ceilings, G-lock status, propagation counter.
Slot 6.  Rolling conversation history appended at send time. For F2 add: do not reference the human's prior turn.
Slot 7.  Output request: generate exactly one turn, stop after one turn. For interrupt mode Slot 2 includes the partial utterance and Slot 3 starts with the F1 floor-grab token.
Engineering Gotchas
F, E1, G3 bypass content filters.  These have no hostile propositional content or a calm surface form. Tag by AVCT code from the researcher's selection only.
F3 needs API max_tokens=15.  Prompting alone will not enforce brevity. Set it in the API call at +2/+3.
Anti-polar seeds must be written separately.  Do not negate antagonistic seeds — that produces hedged antagonism. Write affirmative supportive seeds per category.
D1 at −3 is deliberate sycophancy.  The robot will validate false claims. Flag this in the UI and require confirmation.
M2 Hedged + intensity +3 = sarcasm.  Show a UI warning — this produces an E1 effect regardless of which category is selected.
M4 on D2 defaults to Norm/Policy.  No Justification on a D2 refusal at +2/+3 is a design mistake if unintentional. Make it an explicit opt-in.
